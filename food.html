Debath and response

During the natural resocures project we had to make research about topics here are my notes for the debate and reponse.

  • In the 1850s, the main determinant of a country's quality is its geography.
  • Despite being the 6th largest country in the world, Australia has a relatively small population of 26 million.
  • The main reason the British settled in Australia was because America stopped taking convicts from them in 1782.
  • There were only five places that were good for settlement in Australia, due to their proximity to water for trade and control of waterways.
  • 85% of Australia's population is concentrated in or near these five cities (Perth, Adelaide, Sydney, Brisbane, and Melbourne), which only make up 0.01% of the country's land.
  • Australia is not well-suited for large-scale human settlement due to its desert terrain, dangerous animals and insects, and lack of rainfall in the west.
  • The Great Dividing Range, Australia's largest and longest chain of mountains, also contributes to the lack of rainfall in the country.
  • The Murray Darling Basin is Australia's most significant river system, but it is not ideal for a large civilization due to its lack of rainfall.
  • Only 6% of Australia's land is arable, making farming difficult.
  • Australia was first discovered by the Dutch in 1606 but was not settled permanently until the British arrived with a population of 650,000, while Asia and Europe had populations of 650,000,000 and 150,000,000 respectively.

Crane research

Similiar to my previous project here is some of my crane research notes

  • The research focuses on tower cranes and the ways they are built to handle high winds and weight distribution
  • Tower cranes are built with trusses to handle torsion, have counterweights to balance the weight being lifted, and have pulleys to increase lifting capacity
  • Compound pulleys or block and tackle pulleys can handle more weight because they redirect force multiple times
  • A 10:1 gear ratio is necessary to achieve the required timing in testing
  • Gears and pulleys are used in a variety of settings, including in automobiles and heavy machinery, to control torque and speed.
