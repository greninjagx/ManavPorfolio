Black history month prenstation


What was this

For this assgiment I tried to create a story about Booker T. Washington's rise to sucess.

Why did I choose this

  • Detailed knowledge of historical figures: My prenstation provides an in-depth analysis of Booker T. Washington's life, beliefs, and influence. It discusses his views on political activism, his approach to achieving racial equality, and his impact on education and civil rights. This shows a deep understanding of Washington's role in history and his influence on subsequent generations.
  • Critical Thinking: The prenstation doesn't just present facts, it critically examines Washington's beliefs and strategies. It discusses the controversies and criticisms surrounding his approach, and it analyzes the effectiveness of his strategies in achieving progress for African Americans.
  • Understanding of Historical Context: The prentation shows an understanding of the historical context in which Washington lived and worked. It discusses the challenges faced by African Americans during this period, the different approaches to achieving racial equality, and the debates and controversies surrounding these issues.

Black history essay


What is this

After the black history month prenstation we were asked to create a essay based on what we learned. I Used point, proof, comment format.

Why I choose this

  • Detailed Knowledge of Historical Figures:The essay provides a thorough analysis of key figures in black history, including Martin Luther King Jr., Booker T. Washington, and Ruby Bridges. It discusses their personal sacrifices, their contributions to the fight for racial equality, and the impact of their actions on future generations.
  • Understanding of Historical Context:The essay shows an understanding of the historical context in which these individuals lived and worked. It discusses the challenges they faced, including discrimination, violence, and opposition from both within and outside their communities.
  • Analysis of Historical Impact:The essay analyzes the impact of these individuals' sacrifices on the progress of the civil rights movement and the African American community.
