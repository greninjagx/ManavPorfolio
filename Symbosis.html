Muturalisum

Lion tail maques, Horbills and trees

Here are a few ways how symbiosis is shown in the amazon. First, Trees of the amazon with hornbills and lion-tailed macaques. The lion-tailed macaques feed on the seeds. Doing so will distribute the seeds which will unscarthed with there droppings. Though not many monkeys travel very far. Seeds need to travel far to get bigger. They do this using wings. Hornbills fly all over the amazon. The trees want the hornbills to come and eat at there eat at them therefore the trees have adapted to look unique. This was a example of mutualism.

Immature Leaf bugs and gray mouse lemurs

Immature leaf bugs extract the sap from trees and use it as food. They exercet the rest in the form of honey dew. Gray mouse lemurs then jump in and lick up all of the honey dew. This is a example of commensalism. Finally, Pitcher plants and mountain treeshrew. The pitcher plant has sugar under its lid the mountain tree shrew would lick the sugar. It will then poop fertilizer for the plant. This is a example of mulatisum. These were some symbiosis scenarios in the amazon.
